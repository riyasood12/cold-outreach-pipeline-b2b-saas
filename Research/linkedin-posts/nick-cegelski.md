## Post 1
Date: April 10, 2023

URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/nick-cegelski_how-to-be-a-cold-email-machine-1-forward-activity-7051252651245662208-Htp9

Summary: A 5-point cold email checklist: forward the email to a friend (if they can't immediately grasp what you do, simplify), match social proof to prospect size/industry, avoid proactive objection handling that wastes real estate, simplify your signature (fancy ones get routed to spam), and never ask ChatGPT generic pain point questions or your email will sound remarkably generic.

Key Takeaway: Contact strategy — sequence structure, touch density, channel mix — is irrelevant if the messaging fails once it actually reaches the prospect.

## Post 3
Date: April 17, 2023

URL: https://in.linkedin.com/posts/nick-cegelski_there-was-a-time-when-cheesy-breakup-emails-activity-7053752942650089472-sneH

Summary: Declares gimmicky breakup emails finished and gives a 5-part professional alternative: take blame for failing to resonate, re-explain why you originally reached out, ask to be corrected, push for delegation upward if prospecting C-suite, and use tentative language throughout — includes a full worked example.

Key Takeaway: A well-crafted breakup email takes responsibility and leaves a door open; a badly written one damages the relationship and closes any future re-engagement.

Category: Cold Email Sequencing / Outbound Workflow Audible

## Post 4
Date: ~Early 2023

URL: https://sk.linkedin.com/posts/nick-cegelski_i-was-researching-your-company-and-was-very-activity-7033570721142362112-JNEd

Summary: Calls out "I was researching your company and was very impressed" as fake personalization that signals to the recipient that no real research was done — argues genuine, verifiable research-based openers are the only differentiator in a flooded inbox.

Key Takeaway: Generic admiration openers are as invisible as "I hope this email finds you well" — the only working personalization is something the prospect can verify is specifically about them.

Category: Cold Email Personalization / Prospecting Messaging LinkedIn

## Post 5
Date: ~2024–2025 (exact date requires manual retrieval)

URL: https://www.linkedin.com/in/nick-cegelski/ (direct post URL not captured — find via recent activity)

Summary: Describes using a physical gift in a bubble mailer as the most reliable method for breaking into Tier 1 enterprise accounts at Levelset — research the prospect deeply first, send the package, follow up by email when it ships, follow up again after it lands, and recognise the follow-up is where the meeting actually gets booked.

Key Takeaway: When every digital channel is saturated and automated, a handwritten note in a bubble mailer earns disproportionate attention — effort and relevance still win, just via a different medium.

## Cross-Cutting Themes

Messaging quality over contact volume — every post treats the underlying message as the rate-limiting variable; sequence structure, mailers, and breakup emails all fail if the copy doesn't earn the reader's time

Specificity as the only real differentiator — fake personalization, irrelevant social proof, and ChatGPT-generated pain points are consistently named as the fastest ways to blend in; real research is the only escape

Deliberate effort as a competitive moat — Post 5 makes this explicit: most reps won't spend 30 minutes researching a prospect or writing a card, which is exactly why doing it works
