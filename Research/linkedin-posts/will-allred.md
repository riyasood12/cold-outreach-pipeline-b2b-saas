Date: ~June 12, 2026 

URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/williamallred_i-turned-a-founder-away-from-buying-our-product-activity-7471167673960325122-3KSJ 

Summary: Turned a founder away from buying Lavender because his unit economics didn't support cold email—at $90/mo with a 2-month trial, cold outreach at $900 per paying customer meant a 10-month payback period, while ads converting at $45/trial would cut payback to 5 months. 

Key Takeaway: The channel that got your first 100 users is rarely the channel that gets the next 1,000—calculate CAC and payback period per channel before scaling cold email.

## Post 2

Date: ~April 11, 2026 

URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/williamallred_people-with-bad-offer-outbound-is-dead-activity-7447972849602039808-N1MZ 

Summary: "Outbound is dead" is the refrain from people with bad offers, bad targeting, bad messaging, or no product-market fit—outbound in a vacuum still works if strategy, product, messaging, and targeting are sound, and it works even better paired with strong branding, demand gen, and product marketing. 

Key Takeaway: Outbound exposes every weakness in your go-to-market—fix the offer, targeting, and messaging before blaming the channel.

URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/williamallred_idk-what-pipeline-nerd-needs-to-hear-this-activity-7447614319493722112-Y0YE 

Summary: Inbound, signals, and intent data are all just context for starting a conversation—sometimes the context alone drives the outreach (automation), sometimes it needs further contextualization (hiring, funding, job changes), and sometimes it means jumping on a call or helping them learn more. 

Key Takeaway: Signals are conversation starters, not scripts—match your outreach type and depth to the strength and nature of the context you have.

## Post 4

Date: ~December 8, 2025 

URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/williamallred_common-belief-aes-dont-want-to-prospect-activity-7403799289841217536-x2OK 

Summary: AE-generated pipeline converts 2-4x higher than other sources, but AEs reject SEPs because prospecting is a one-off task for them and the tools feel like a Ferrari when they need a bike—the fix is pre-researching accounts, teeing up drafts, and making prospecting a quick daily review instead of a chore. 

Key Takeaway: AEs don't hate prospecting—they hate the workflow; give them pre-drafted emails with research riding shotgun and prospecting becomes a fast daily activity instead of an ordeal. 

Category: SDR Workflows

## Post 5

Date: ~December 4, 2025 

URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/williamallred_be-honest-how-many-of-yall-have-an-outreach-activity-7402391741829455872-44tH 

Summary: Gave Ora a list of emails, templates with bracketed fields, and rules for filling them—AI filled in personalization from its own research, hitting 9.5% reply rate and 50% positive reply rate on a cold campaign, with emails pushable into Outreach/SalesLoft/Gong as manual or automated steps. 

Key Takeaway: AI agents that fill structured templates with deep research outperform blank-slate generation—give the agent a framework and let it personalize within guardrails. 

Category: Cold Email

## Cross-Cutting Themes

Theme 1: Outbound failure is almost always a fundamentals problem (offer, targeting, messaging, unit economics)—not a channel problem

Theme 2: Workflow design determines adoption—AEs and AI agents both perform better when the system tees up work instead of requiring blank-page effort

Theme 3: Context and research depth are the dividing line—signals must be translated into relevant messaging, not just referenced
