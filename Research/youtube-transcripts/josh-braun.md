# Josh Braun

The two most valuable Josh Braun videos for understanding modern B2B SaaS outbound are his Feb 2026 interview on outbound philosophy and his May 2026 cold-calling opener breakdown, because together they cover why outbound now requires disqualification, offer design, permission-based openers, and human conversation rather than scripted selling.

1) Josh Braun on Outbound: Why It's Not Your Job to Sell to Everyone

URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uT6uIAAgFGU

Date: February 4, 2026

Transcript Summary:
- Josh argues that modern outbound is not about selling to everyone, but about helping the right people decide whether your solution is genuinely relevant for them.

- He says many outbound failures come from trying to convince buyers who do not actually have the problem you solve, which wastes time and damages trust.

- He emphasizes early disqualification in conversations, asking questions that surface whether the buyer has the right motivations, budget, and decision process.

- He stresses that outbound should be about starting useful conversations, not pushing a pitch, and that the goal is to help buyers evaluate fit.

- Josh critiques the old “more volume = more pipeline” mindset, arguing that volume without relevance and qualification creates noise and low-quality meetings.

- He recommends designing outbound around buyer realities: short attention windows, skepticism of sales, and the need for clear, non-salesy relevance.

Key Insights
- Modern outbound is a qualification-heavy system: your job is to help buyers decide fit, not to sell universally.

- Early disqualification and honest conversation design materially improve meeting quality and reduce wasted cycles.

- Outbound systems that prioritize relevance and fit over volume adapt better to today’s skeptical, busy buyers.

2) The Best Non-salesy Cold Call Opener (Ft. Josh Braun)

URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OJ2VUIlpJfQ

Date: May 10, 2026

Transcript Summary:
- Josh explains that most cold callers start with salesy openers that trigger immediate defensiveness, which breaks the conversation in the first 10 seconds.

- He advocates a non-salesy, permission-based opener that acknowledges the awkwardness of cold calling and asks for permission to continue briefly.

- He frames the opener around respect: “I know this is a random call… I’d like 30 seconds to see if this is worth continuing.”

- He emphasizes that the opener should be honest, human, and transparent about the call’s purpose, rather than hiding behind a fake reason or scripted hook.

- Josh argues that when you give buyers an explicit choice to stop or continue, they feel more in control and are more willing to engage honestly.

- He contrasts this with traditional openers that try to impress with stats, buzz, or product claims, which usually backfire with skeptical buyers.

- He stresses that the opener is the foundation for a conversation that can move into real problems, not just a pitch.

Key Insights
- A non-salesy, permission-based opener that acknowledges the cold call and offers a choice is more effective than scripted, salesy hooks.

- Giving buyers control in the first 10 seconds reduces defensiveness and increases the chance of a genuine conversation.

- Openers that are honest and human build more trust than openers that try to impress or hide the sales intent.

Why These Two Videos?
These videos together show Josh Braun’s modern outbound system:

- The outbound philosophy video explains why outbound must be qualification-driven, relevance-focused, and conversation-based rather than pitch-driven.

- The cold call opener video shows how to execute that philosophy tactically in the most critical moment: the first 10–30 seconds of a call.

Together, they cover both the mindset (disqualify, help buyers decide) and the mechanics (permission-based, non-salesy openers) that define Josh Braun’s approach to modern B2B SaaS outbound.
