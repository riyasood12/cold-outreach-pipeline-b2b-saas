The two most valuable Jason Bay videos for understanding modern B2B SaaS outbound systems are his short messaging masterclass and his long-form cold-calling framework interview, because together they cover message design, offer creation, call structure, coaching, metrics, and multichannel execution in the current outbound environment.

Video picks
1) How to Build a Perfect Outbound Sales Pitch with Jason Bay

URL:  https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IywS6oZwKq4

Date: Oct 25, 2024 (published Oct 25, 2024)

Transcript Summary
- Jason says outbound messaging fails when it is written by siloed teams that do not regularly talk to customers.

- He recommends building a cross-functional “messaging team” with top reps, sales leaders, marketing, and subject-matter experts.

- He argues that good messaging starts with customer priorities, status quo, problems, and aspirations rather than product language.

- He uses contact-center leaders as an example, showing that reps should speak in customer voice, such as reducing cost to serve, instead of pitching features.

- He stresses tighter segmentation by persona and industry so reps start with messaging that is already 80–90% relevant.

- He suggests improving messaging by workshopping with top reps, running customer fireside chats, and extracting patterns from recorded calls.

- He says the offer matters as much as the message, and that buyers need something valuable in return for their time, such as an audit or benchmark insight.

Key Insights
- Strong outbound systems begin with message architecture, not sequence volume.

- Customer-language extraction from real calls is a core system input for better outbound.

- “Give-to-get” offers make cold outreach more credible and materially improve meeting quality.

Second video
2) Cold Calling Isn't Dead, BUT You Need To Be Top 20% To Make It Work - @jasondbay

URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4TuHuaZe0bU

Date: Jan 9, 2026

Transcript Summary
- Jason says cold calling is harder than it was a few years ago, but still works for top teams when enablement, data quality, and execution are strong.

- He cites benchmark gaps between average and top performers across connect rates, conversion rates, and show rates.

- He argues that the biggest outbound upgrade now is the offer, because generic “let’s meet” requests no longer earn attention.

- He explains the permission-based opener as a way to survive the first 80 seconds of a cold call and quickly earn attention.

- He follows the opener with a “reverse pitch,” where reps talk about buyer problems in customer language instead of describing the product.

- He emphasizes using real customer conversations, call transcripts, and recent sales calls to sharpen outbound language.

- For leaders, he lays out a system for weekly outbound rhythm, rep-level coaching, and diagnosing issues by activity, connect rate, call quality, conversion, and show rate.

Key Insights
- Modern outbound systems are operational systems as much as messaging systems; call outcomes depend on data quality, timing, coaching, and metrics.

- The best reps win by combining relevance, context, and problem-based language rather than relying on brand or scripts alone.

- Jason’s framework is especially valuable because it links frontline tactics to manager-led inspection and coaching loops.

Why these two?
These two videos are the best pair because one explains how to design outbound messaging and offers, while the other shows how to execute and manage outbound at the rep and team level. Together, they give a practical picture of Jason Bay’s current outbound system for B2B SaaS: segment tightly, speak in customer language, make a compelling offer, use structured calls, and coach the motion through measurable stages.
