# Eric Nowoslawski

## Video 1
Title: EP 02 Eric Nowoslawski (Growth Engine X): The Ultimate Cold Email Tech Stack (ft. Clay & Smartlead)

URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7ttZEC6khZ0

Channel: Smartlead- Behind the Agency Podcast

Date: September 19, 2025

Transcript Summary:

- Signal-based prospecting powers smarter outreach using live triggers instead of static Apollo/ZoomInfo lists to reach prospects at the moment a problem is most likely active.
- Clay handles enrichment and AI personalisation; Smartlead handles inbox rotation, bulk sends, and unified analytics, the two tools solve different problems in sequence, not interchangeably.
- Domain architecture and deliverability at scale: rotating across aged domains with 30–50 sends per inbox per day across multiple IP subnets, sustaining 1.5M+ emails/month without burning sender reputation.
- Full live workflow walkthrough: ICP-scored list built in Clay → AI copy snippets generated per row → pushed to Smartlead with inbox rotation pre-configured → reply rates and deliverability monitored in Master Inbox → winning segments fed back into Clay.
- Deliverability at volume is a systems problem backup inbox pools, daily bounce monitoring with a 3% hard stop, and inbox health dashboards must be designed before the first send
- Bad data upstream multiplies at scale a 5% invalid-address rate at 1.5M sends/month means 75,000 emails hitting dead addresses monthly, progressively degrading the entire domain pool

Key Insights:

- Clay and Smartlead are complem  entary layers, not substitutes and each solves a distinct stage of the outbound system
- Deliverability infrastructure must be built before campaigns launch, not retrofitted after performance degrades
- Signals change who you contact and when, not what you say about conflating signals with personalisation produces well-timed but generically messaged outreach


## Video 2
Title: EP04 Eric Nowoslawski (Growth Engine X): What Actually Works - Campaign Breakdowns

URL: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WmrYeN3GE3w

Channel: Smartlead - Behind the Agency Podcast

Date: October 14, 2025

Transcript Summary:

- Campaign architecture over execution on  breaking down everything from ICP and messaging to results, covering the QA process via Master Inbox, A/B testing wins, and how to replicate proven frameworks across verticals.
- ICP defined as a scoring rubric (weighted firmographic + technographic + behavioural signals), not a persona description - only accounts above a threshold enter any campaign
- Secureframe case study: three structurally distinct messaging frameworks for CTOs (speed to compliance), CFOs (cost vs. hiring), and VPs of Engineering (integration depth) - same product, three different opening arguments, none mentioning the product name in email
- Website Closers case study: a competitor keyword in the subject line was triggering spam filters - switching to a problem-led subject line reduced spam placement by 60% with no other changes
- QA checklist before every campaign goes live: deliverability score per inbox, spam-word scan, preview text rendering across Gmail/Outlook, and manual review of 10 randomly sampled personalised emails to catch AI output errors
- A/B testing priority: offer angle first (500 send minimum) → subject line → opening sentence → CTA last. Testing copy before validating the offer optimises the execution layer of a broken campaign
- Campaign playbook of proven types (Creative Ideas, Trigger-Based, Lead Magnet, Free Trial, Competitor Displacement) — new types only added after - positive reply rates across two independent client tests in different verticals 

Key Insights:

- Campaign architecture is a ranked hierarchy- ICP → offer → copy → test, in that order; collapsing stages makes failure variables unknowable
- Persona-specific messaging is structural, not tonal - the Secureframe case shows each buyer needs a different opening argument, not just different wording
- Manual QA of a random sample before launch catches AI personalisation errors that aggregate dashboards never surface
