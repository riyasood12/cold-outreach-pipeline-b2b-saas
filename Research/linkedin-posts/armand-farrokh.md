# Armand Farrokh

## Post 1
Date: April 10, 2023

URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/armand-farrokh_sdr-salesdevelopment-activity-7051225038154334208-cHJW

Summary: Introduces the 3x3 Email Rule : no longer than 3 bodies of text, 3 lines each, built for mobile reading, structured as: tailoring + problem to one-sentence solution to low-friction CTA.

Key Takeaway: Cold email brevity is a structural discipline in which every section has a job, and the CTA should invite interest, not demand a meeting.

## Post 2
Date: ~2022

URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/armand-farrokh_sales-sdr-coldcalling-activity-6901952603966599168-h_gF

Summary: Shows how to use 4 company-level signals (funding round, employee growth, job postings, CEO blog post) as cold email hooks but only when each trigger is tied to a specific, relevant problem, not dropped as a generic congratulation.

Key Takeaway: Triggers only stand out when attached to a problem, everyone sends "congrats on the round," so yours needs to say what that funding means for the prospect's actual challenges.

Category: Cold Email Personalization / Trigger-Based Outreach YouTube

## Post 3
Date: ~September 2022

URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/armand-farrokh_coldcalling-prospecting-activity-6979114887734046720-h6or

Summary: Covers three mechanical adjustments that fix cold call tone: speak uncomfortably slowly (calm confidence), eliminate upward inflection (not too eager), and laugh at tough objections (shows comfort under pressure).

Key Takeaway: Tone mechanics not scripts , separate cold calls that get heard from ones that get immediately hung up on.

Category: Cold Call Execution / SDR Workflow BlitzMetrics

## Post 4
Date: ~September 2022

URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/armand-farrokh_three-ways-to-open-a-cold-call-1-call-activity-6980622082178969601-xG9c

Summary: Argues common openers ("how's your day going," "did I catch you at a bad time") make you sound like every other cold caller; introduces the "Context First Opener" front-loading one specific researched fact about the prospect before anything else.

Key Takeaway: The only thing that can be unique about your opener is the prospect themselves and everything else is noise.

Category: Cold Call Openers / Prospecting Messaging Alexberman

## Post 5
Date: ~June 2021

URL: https://www.linkedin.com/posts/armand-farrokh_30mpc-sdr-salesdevelopment-activity-6808453584800370689-w8jX/

Summary: Breaks down cold email preview text strategy,  the first 14 words must lead with what you know about the prospect (job posting, funding, portfolio signal), not with the rep's name, company, or preambles like "just circling back."

Key Takeaway: Preview text is your real subject line if it opens with "Armand from Acme," it telegraphs "sales email" and kills the open before it starts.

Category: Cold Email Copywriting / Outbound Messaging LinkedIn

## Cross-Cutting Themes

Specificity over volume , every post punishes generic content and rewards one specific, researched detail about the individual prospect
Friction reduction as strategy from interest-based CTAs to low-pressure call tone, reducing the perceived cost of engagement consistently outperforms increased persuasion
Structure first, then personalization is the 3x3 rule, Context First Opener, and trigger frameworks all share the same logic: lock in the structure, then slot personalization in
